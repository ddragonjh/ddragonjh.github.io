Editorial and correction policy

Fix Atlas publishes technical troubleshooting material for people who are already blocked by an exact error. This page explains how the material is selected, sourced, reviewed, and corrected.

Selection and intent

Topics are selected from recurring error phrases, official support documentation, product status incidents, and reader correction requests. A page must address an observable failure and provide a diagnostic order. We do not publish invented product errors, fake benchmarks, or pages whose only purpose is to repeat a keyword.

Sources and verification

Product documentation, standards, and maintainers' repositories are preferred over unsourced forum answers. Every exact-error page links to a primary reference. Commands are checked for syntax and placed after the evidence they are intended to collect. Because operating systems and product versions differ, publication does not claim that every command has been executed in every possible environment.

Safety standard

Reversible checks come before destructive changes. Pages avoid broad permission changes, untrusted downloads, secret disclosure, and data deletion as casual fixes. When a command can expose credentials or remove state, the page must warn the reader or recommend a safer diagnostic alternative.

Automation and human responsibility

Automated tools may assist with research organization, drafting, link checks, and formatting. Publication responsibility remains with Fix Atlas. Pages are structured around primary references and exact product behavior, and automation is not treated as a source. Reader reports are reviewed against current documentation before a change is made.

Corrections and updates

Material changes update the reviewed date. Broken sources are replaced with the current official location. Corrections can be sent through the contact page with the page URL, exact disputed statement, product version, and a supporting primary source. Substantive errors are corrected directly; we do not charge for corrections or removal of inaccurate claims.
